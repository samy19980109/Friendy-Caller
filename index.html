About Me

I am a highschool student living in the GTA, that has been self-isolated along with my fellow peers. However, hearing the news about seniors in Ontario, and their struggle with social-distancing and Covid-19, sparked the inspiration to help those seniors who would be going through a difficult time. I started this charitable program called The Friendly Caller, to help seniors who are feeling lonely and scared during this challenging time, by providing methods of socialization while adhering to the protocols of social distancing.

Getting started

For the first step, I decided to contact a number of retirement homes in the GTA to see if there was any interest in this program, and if they would be willing to participate. I received positive feedback from a number of homes, as well as new suggestions, such as designing uplifting posters. After a few homes expressed their interest, I decided to recruit volunteers from my highschool to design posters, and to participate in the caller program.

To date, I am currently collaborating with four retirement homes with posters, of which two will also be participating in the caller program. My goal is to be able to provide support for an additional ten retirement homes by June 2020.

My Work

Posters

The posters are designed with both words and visuals, to help motivate seniors, and keep them thinking positively during these times. The messages on the posters include quotes from famous scholars and historical figures (Plato, Oscar Wilde, etc.). The visuals have been carefully designed to strengthen the messages.

Caller Program

The caller program pairs volunteers with seniors living in retirement homes facing self-isolation, for one on one weekly phone conversations. Volunteers are largely high school students looking to give back to the community. Research has shown that seniors who stay socially connected have experienced an improvement in their health as well as better intellectual stimulation and cognition and reduced emotional & physical pain. In order to help seniors keep their health up while following social distancing, this program offers a fun alternative for seniors looking for some company during this isolating time.
